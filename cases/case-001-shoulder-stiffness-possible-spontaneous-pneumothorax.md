# Case 001: Shoulder stiffness or possible spontaneous pneumothorax?

## Status

Synthetic educational case.  
Not a real patient case.  
Not a de-identified case report.  
Not an adverse event database entry.

## Category

Delayed referral / Red flags / Boundary between acupuncture care and medical assessment

## Synthetic scenario

A young adult male with a slim body type and a smoking history contacts an acupuncture practitioner because of sudden shoulder stiffness and upper back discomfort.

At first, the complaint sounds like ordinary shoulder stiffness or muscle tension. However, the practitioner asks whether there is anything unusual about the pain. The person then says that the discomfort began suddenly, is mainly on one side, and feels sharper when taking a deep breath. He also describes mild shortness of breath.

The practitioner does not treat or explain the symptoms as ordinary shoulder stiffness, posture-related muscle tension, autonomic imbalance, or qi stagnation. Instead, the practitioner explains that sudden one-sided shoulder or upper back discomfort with breathing symptoms may indicate a chest or respiratory problem and recommends prompt medical assessment.

## Patient safety issue

Some potentially serious chest or respiratory conditions may initially be described as shoulder stiffness, upper back discomfort, or vague musculoskeletal pain.

The safety issue is not whether acupuncture caused the problem. The issue is whether the practitioner recognizes that the complaint may fall outside the safe scope of acupuncture care and may require medical assessment.

## Decision point

The key decision point is when the complaint shifts from ordinary musculoskeletal discomfort to possible chest or respiratory involvement.

Warning features include:

- sudden onset
- one-sided shoulder, upper back, or chest-area discomfort
- pain that becomes sharper with deep breathing
- shortness of breath
- unusual severity or unfamiliar quality of pain
- feeling faint, unusually weak, or clearly unwell

## Safer response

A safer response is to avoid treating or reassuring the person as if this were ordinary shoulder stiffness.

The practitioner should recommend prompt medical assessment when sudden one-sided shoulder or upper back discomfort is accompanied by breathing symptoms or pain with deep breathing.

The practitioner should also avoid saying that the symptoms are likely due to posture, stress, autonomic imbalance, qi stagnation, or a normal musculoskeletal problem unless more serious causes have been appropriately considered.

## Prevention points

- Do not treat all shoulder or upper back complaints as musculoskeletal pain.
- Ask whether there is chest pain, shortness of breath, sudden onset, one-sided symptoms, or pain with breathing.
- If respiratory or chest-related symptoms are present, recommend medical assessment.
- Traditional or musculoskeletal explanations should not delay evaluation of possible serious conditions.
- Safety advice should be documented when possible.
