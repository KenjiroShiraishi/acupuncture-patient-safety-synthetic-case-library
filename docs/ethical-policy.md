# Ethical Policy

This project publishes only synthetic educational cases for acupuncture patient safety.

The purpose of this project is to support learning from near misses, adverse-event concerns, delayed referral, communication problems, informed consent issues, and safety-related decision points in acupuncture practice.

## Core principle

Real patient cases are not published in this repository.

This project does not publish:

- real patient records
- raw clinical notes
- original incident reports
- de-identified real case reports
- identifiable patient information
- facility-specific information
- dates, locations, names, occupations, or rare details that may enable re-identification
- unreviewed AI-generated outputs

## Synthetic case policy

All public cases are fictionalized or synthetic educational cases.

They are created from abstracted learning points, not from raw clinical records.

The cases should not be interpreted as real case reports, epidemiological data, or evidence of incidence or frequency.

## Human review

Before publication, each synthetic case should be reviewed by a human reviewer.

The reviewer should check whether:

- the case could be mistaken for a real patient case
- any details may allow re-identification
- the case includes unnecessary personal or facility-specific details
- the clinical safety lesson is clear
- the case does not blame patients, practitioners, schools, or facilities
- the risk is not exaggerated
- the risk is not minimized

## AI use

AI may be used to help transform abstracted learning points into synthetic educational cases.

AI is not used as a substitute for privacy protection, ethical judgment, clinical judgment, or human review.

## Purpose limitation

This project is not intended to:

- advertise acupuncture
- criticize individual practitioners
- evaluate the frequency of adverse events
- provide medical advice
- replace clinical training or medical consultation

The purpose is patient safety education.
