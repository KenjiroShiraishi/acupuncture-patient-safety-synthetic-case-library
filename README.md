# Acupuncture Patient Safety Synthetic Case Library

A synthetic educational case library for acupuncture patient safety.

This repository provides fictionalized/synthetic educational cases focused on near misses, adverse-event concerns, delayed referral, communication, informed consent, follow-up, and safer clinical practice in acupuncture settings.

This is not a collection of real patient cases.

## Purpose

The purpose of this project is to support patient safety learning in acupuncture practice.

In acupuncture and related traditional medicine settings, many discussions focus on successful cases, techniques, theories, or practitioner experience. However, patient safety also requires learning from uncertainty, near misses, possible adverse events, delayed referral, inadequate explanation, and situations where reassessment or medical consultation may have been needed.

This project aims to convert abstracted safety learning points into synthetic educational cases that can be shared publicly without exposing real patients, practitioners, facilities, or raw clinical records.

## What this repository contains

This repository may include:

- synthetic educational cases
- patient safety learning points
- case templates
- AI prompts used for fictionalized case generation
- review checklists
- documentation on ethical handling of safety cases
- publication preparation materials

## What this repository does not contain

This repository does not publish:

- real patient cases
- raw clinical notes
- original incident reports
- de-identified real case reports
- identifiable patient information
- facility-specific information
- dates, locations, names, or rare details that could enable re-identification
- unreviewed AI-generated outputs

## Important note

The cases in this repository are synthetic educational materials.

They are not real case reports, not clinical evidence of incidence or frequency, and not an adverse event database. They should not be used to estimate how often a particular event occurs in acupuncture practice.

The purpose is educational: to help practitioners, students, researchers, and educators think about safer clinical decision-making.

## Method

The basic workflow is:

1. A safety learning point is abstracted by a human reviewer.
2. Identifiable details are removed before any public writing.
3. AI may be used to transform the abstracted learning point into a fictionalized educational case.
4. A human reviewer checks the case before publication.
5. Only the synthetic educational case is published.

AI is not used as a substitute for ethical review, clinical judgment, or privacy protection.

## Case structure

Each synthetic case may include:

- case title
- category
- synthetic scenario
- patient safety issue
- decision point
- safer response
- prevention points
- reflection questions
- public note

## Categories

Cases may address topics such as:

- near misses
- suspected adverse events
- delayed referral
- red flags
- explanation and informed consent
- communication failure
- follow-up failure
- boundary between acupuncture care and medical care
- patient anxiety and power imbalance
- risk of false reassurance

## Ethical policy

This project follows the principle that patient safety learning should not require public exposure of real patients or practitioners.

The project does not aim to blame individual practitioners, criticize specific schools or styles, or promote acupuncture. Its purpose is to support safer, more accountable, and more reflective practice.

## AI use

AI tools may be used to help draft synthetic educational cases from abstracted learning elements.

All AI-assisted outputs must be reviewed by a human before publication.

The prompts used for case generation and safety review may be made publicly available in this repository to support transparency and reproducibility.

## Medical disclaimer

This repository is for educational purposes only.

It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or legal advice. Clinical decisions should be made by qualified professionals based on individual circumstances, relevant regulations, and appropriate medical consultation.
